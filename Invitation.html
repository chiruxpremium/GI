A Letter From My Heart
My Dearest Kalpana Amma
— with love, gratitude, and a son's heart —
❦

There are some people whose love quietly raises us —
without asking for thanks, without keeping count —
and you, Amma, are one of them.

When we were far away from home, far away from our mother's kitchen and our family's warmth, you opened your doors and your heart to us. The dosa batters you sent, the curries you packed with so much care, the meals you made sure we never missed — Amma, those weren't just food. They were home. Every spoonful carried your love, your blessings, and the comfort of a mother's hand. On the days we felt the distance from home the most, you made sure we never felt alone.

You took care of us the way only a mother does — gently, completely, and without ever expecting anything in return. You became our second mother in a country that was new to us, and in doing so, you became one of the reasons I could keep going, keep studying, and keep believing that this day would come.

And now that day is here.

It is with so much love and gratitude that I want you to be a part of this moment with me. I am graduating from Stevens Institute of Technology, and I would be truly blessed if you could come and bless me in person as I walk across the stage.

✦ You are warmly invited ✦
Graduate Commencement Ceremony
Stevens Institute of Technology · Class of 2026
Wednesday, May 20, 2026 · 3:00 PM
PNC Bank Arts Center
116 Garden State Pkwy, Holmdel, NJ 07733

I will arrange your ticket and share all the details with you personally.
Please just say yes, Amma.

❦

This degree may have my name on it, but a piece of it belongs to you — to your kitchen, to your prayers, and to every meal you fed us with a mother's love. Walking the stage will feel complete only when I look up and see you there.

With all my love and pranams,
Chiru
✦   your son, always   ✦
